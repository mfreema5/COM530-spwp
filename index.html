PSYC 380 “Advanced Statistics” at IIT

PSED II regression response-surface

I'm interested in the (mis)use of multiple regression analysis in academic papers, so I took PSYC 380 ‘Advanced Statistics – Applied Correlation and Regression’ at IIT. Since it was an undergraduate level course, the credit hours I earned from it can't be applied toward my Master's degree, but I still think it was worth the time as I learned quite a lot about properly applying various regression and correlation methods.

Indeed, for my final project for PSYC 380, I did an analysis of data based on a hypothesis developed by Maija Renko, Associate Professor of Entrepreneurship at UIC. The data came from the Panel Study of Entrepreneurial Dynamics II (PSED-II). I employed a response-surface analysis technique (per Shanock, et al. (2010)), and the results were promising enough that professor Renko has received research funding to expand it into a research article.

Working with professor of entrepreneurship at UIC to expand into research paper the statistical analysis done as project for ‘Advanced Statistics – Applied Correlation and Regression’ IIT course.
